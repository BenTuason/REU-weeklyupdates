Week 1

I got to my dorm around 2:30pm and finished moving everything in my dorm at 4. This week we did orientation, picture day, meeting our advisors, and our first seminar. My advisor is Dr. Rakesh Verma, and everybody in the department does some sort of data related project (ML & AI). Dr. Verma does research in NLP and cybersecurity like detecting hate crime, scam email detection, and protecting ML models. The main project that intersted me is trying to come up with an PAC-learnable, efficient, and scalable linear binary separator for negative and postive classes (This is a mouthful, but basically an efficient & scalable SVM for high dimensions). I asked him if it's purely theoretical and he said yes which kinda excited me since I've been really interested in theoretical computer science. From the seminars, my favorite was from Dr. Jaspal Subhlok which was making it easier to summarize lecture recordings with slides. Dr. Verma said he needs to talk with other collaborators if I'm allowed to get in the project to work along side them. I haven't gotten an update yet hopefully week 2 I get one. So right now I've been following Sipser's complexity theory part of his thoery of computation lectures and learning theory lecture slides from CMU. I haven't done any proof yet but I've been reading A LOT of proofs and reading a lot of other mathematically intensive papers which is cool because there's a lot of cool symbols :p But week 2 was super super sucessful and lots of progress on stablizing my foundational understanding of the problem and this is the first step of understanding TCS completely!
